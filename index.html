1. Introduction
The task presented was to use object recognition to count something over time. I think fish swimming look cool, and I was interested in seeing whether the overlapping swimming, where sometimes part of the second fish is obscured by the first, affects the labelling.
I also remembered that YOLO performs better with high quality images with large objects, so I thought the model would perform well on the set of images of these fish swimming: 2 objects taking up over 1 of the 7x7 sections most of the time, and several when fully pictured, and in reasonable quality of 720p.
2. Method
I used the YoloV7 model because I found a tutorial on it that was intuitive to me, and I could model my code on this. Currently, YoloV8 is the latest, state-of-the-art model, but YoloV7 is comparable.

4. Analysis of Results
I learned that fish aren't a priority for training data in the YOLO series, including the YoloV7 model. The model was very confident that the fish was a bird, and consistently. This is strange, and now I wonder: why mark it as a bird instead of not marking it at all? Focusing on the boxing, the overlap did cause problems for the dual-identification success, even at low levels of overlap. Finally, more than half of the fish being out-of-bounds resulted in no identification, most of the time. Overall, I had a lot of fun with this project and learned about some of the limitations of YOLO.
5. Analysis of Vision Algorithms
The model didn't identify the fish as fish, or consistently give the correct quantification. I didn't try to optimize the misidentification because this would involve training the model, and I don't have the knowledge or compute power to do this. I could 'throw out' images where only one box was provided, but this would result in very few images from the original video being included, and wouldn't be useful. I didn't choose another video because I wanted to be honest about my results, but if it is considered preferable to choose input data that fit the model in the future, please let me know and I will be happy to do this.
